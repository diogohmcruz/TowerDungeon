## 2. Premise

The king is murdered by a powerful necromancer who possesses his corpse,
kidnaps Princess Ignelia, and retreats into the Dragon Tower. After the
kingdom's expedition suffers heavy losses, ordinary villagers are
conscripted. One of them is **Yuva**, a farm boy who becomes the story's
protagonist.

The central objective remains constant throughout the story:

- Climb the tower
- Rescue the princess
- Survive

## 3. Main Characters

### Yuva

- Farm boy with no military training
- Strong from years of manual labor
- Learns through survival rather than sudden power-ups
- Notable traits: endurance, courage, adaptability

### Princess Ignelia

- Captured by the necromancer
- Appears deeply connected to the Dragon Tower and its ancient powers
- More than a simple royal hostage

### The Necromancer

- Primary antagonist
- Murdered and possesses the king
- Commands undead, corrupted creatures, and tower guardians
- True objectives remain unknown

### Eliquo

- Captain of the expedition
- Experienced military leader
- Gradually recognizes Yuva's potential

### Sargan

- Fire mage
- Provides ranged magical support
- One of the expedition's strongest combatants

### Ardiellia

- Introduced later in the story
- Possesses knowledge connected to the world's hidden history
- Expands the scope of the narrative beyond the rescue mission

## 4. World Building

Although the setting initially resembles medieval Europe, it gradually
reveals: - Forgotten civilizations - Ancient architecture - Magical
races - Impossible biology - Cosmic-scale mysteries

## 5. The Dragon Tower

The Dragon Tower is effectively its own ecosystem containing: - Vast
chambers - Forests - Rivers - Ruined cities - Bridges - Organic growth -
Magical corruption

The interior appears significantly larger than the exterior.

## 6. Architecture

Nihei emphasizes environmental storytelling through: - Massive halls -
Impossible staircases - Ancient cathedrals - Gigantic bridges - Ruins -
Organic structures

## 7. Magic System

### Elemental Magic

- Offensive spells
- Fire magic
- Magical projectiles

### Necromancy

- Raising undead
- Corpse possession
- Corruption
- Mutation

### Transformation Magic

- Draconic transformations
- Appears innate rather than learned

### Magical Artifacts

- Weapons
- Rings
- Ancient relics

### Ancient Magic

The tower itself seems powered by forgotten magical technology or
civilization.

## 8. Races

### Humans

Most soldiers and civilians.

### Drakmetamorphs

- Dragon-descended humanoids
- Can transform
- Strong magical affinity
- Closely tied to royalty

### Fairies

Small magical beings possessing unique knowledge.

### Giants

Rare but physically powerful.

### Beastfolk

Various anthropomorphic races.

## 9. Monsters

- Undead soldiers
- Giant insects
- Chimeras
- Dragon creatures
- Tower beasts
- Ancient magical constructs

## 10. Combat

Combat emphasizes: - Resource management - Teamwork - Terrain -
Permanent injuries - Weapon degradation

## 11. Themes

- Survival
- Fear of the unknown
- Human perseverance
- Ancient civilizations
- Corruption

## 12. Story Structure

Typical progression: 1. Enter the tower 2. Explore 3. Fight 4. Lose
companions 5. Discover mysteries 6. Climb higher 7. Face increasingly
dangerous enemies

## 13. Strengths

- Creature design
- Environmental storytelling
- Massive architecture
- Slow character growth
- Mystery
- Horror atmosphere
- Constant tension

## 14. Major Mysteries

- Who built the Dragon Tower?
- Why did it descend from the sky?
- What is the necromancer's ultimate goal?
- Why is Princess Ignelia special?
- What are the origins of the Drakmetamorphs?
- Why does the tower distort space?
- What exists at the summit?
